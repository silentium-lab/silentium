Dependency Inversion Principal

DIP is the dependency inversion principle described by Robert Martin. The principle says that the architect of the system can change the direction. procedural dependencies - when more depends on less on the opposite is true when less depends on more. More is higher policy (business logic) and plugins depend on the needs of business logic specific systems, for example, a database plugin that provides our business logic with data from the database.

The Patron library is an implementation of the DIP principle. Your business logic can depend on interfaces sourceType or GuestType or ActionType . These common interfaces allow you to either get the data(sourceType) or accept the one to whom the data needs to be transferred (GuestType) or accept an object that can perform actions(ActionType)
